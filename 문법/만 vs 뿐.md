
#### 만

This is a **particle** that simply restricts or limits, meaning "only/just":

- **한 번만** — "just once / only once"
- Feels more like a **simple limitation**
- Very commonly used in casual speech

---

#### 뿐

This carries a stronger sense of **"nothing more than / that is all"**:

- **한 번뿐** — "only once, and that is it / nothing more"
- Feels more **final and emphatic**
- Slightly more literary or emotional in tone

---

#### In your sentence:

> **졸업식은 평생에 한 번뿐이잖아요**

**뿐** works better here because you are making an **emotional, emphatic point** to persuade your friend — "it only happens once in your whole life, that's it!" The finality of **뿐** strengthens the argument.

**만** would also be understandable:

> **졸업식은 평생에 한 번만 있잖아요**

But it feels slightly less emphatic — more like stating a fact than making an emotional appeal, which is what you need when **persuading** someone!
