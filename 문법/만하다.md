## 만하다 #card
?begin
### Usage
Used to indicate something is worth doing or deserving of something.
Expresses that something has enough value or quality to warrant the action.

### Form
**Present tense:**
- Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ 만하다
- Vowel ending: Verb stem + ㄹ 만하다
- Consonant ending: Verb stem + 을 만하다

**Past tense:**
- Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ 만했다

**Future tense:**
- Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ 만할 것이다

### Restrictions
- Used with action verbs
- Cannot be used with descriptive verbs/adjectives directly
- Common verbs: 가다, 보다, 먹다, 읽다, 믿다, 듣다, 추천하다

### Examples
- 가다 → 갈 만하다 (be worth going)
- 보다 → 볼 만하다 (be worth watching/seeing)
- 먹다 → 먹을 만하다 (be worth eating / edible)
- 믿다 → 믿을 만하다 (be worth trusting / trustworthy)
- 읽다 → 읽을 만하다 (be worth reading)
- 만나다 → 만날 만하다 (be worth meeting)
- 일하다 → 일할 만하다 (be worth working)
- 공부하다 → 공부할 만하다 (be worth studying)
- 추천하다 → 추천할 만하다 (be worth recommending)

#### In sentences:
- 한국에서 가 볼 만한 곳을 좀 추천해 주시겠어요?
	- Can you recommend any places in Korea worth visiting?
- 설악산이 어때요? 지금 가을이라서 단풍이 정말 아름다울 거예요
	- How about Seorak mountain? Since it is autumn now, the fall foliage will be really beautiful
- 수지 씨가 만든 음식이 어땠어요?
	- How was the food Suzy made?
- 모두 맛있었지만 특히 김치찌개는 정말 먹을 만해요
	- All foods were good, but her kimchi stew is really worth trying
- 이 영화는 볼 만해요
	- This movie is worth watching
- 그 사람은 믿을 만한 사람이에요
	- That person is trustworthy (a person worth trusting)

### Notes
- Can be used as an adjective modifier when combined with nouns (e.g., 볼 만한 영화 - a movie worth watching)
- Similar to English expressions: "worth doing", "deserve", "worthwhile"
