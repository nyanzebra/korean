## 바에야 #card
?begin
### Usage
Expresses that the later choice is the better option, although neither of two choices is satisfactory. Means "I would rather do that than do this" or "rather than doing X, I'd prefer Y". Used when comparing two undesirable options and choosing the lesser of two evils.

### Form
**Basic form:**
- Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ 바에야
- Vowel ending: Verb stem + ㄹ 바에야
- Consonant ending: Verb stem + 을 바에야

**Alternative form:**
- Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ 바에는

**Common combinations:**
- -(으)ㄹ 바에야 차라리 (I would rather...)
- -(으)ㄹ 바에야 그냥 (I'd just...)
- -(으)ㄹ 바에야 아예 (I'd completely...)

### Restrictions
- Only used with action verbs
- Both options being compared are typically negative or undesirable
- Can be used interchangeably with -느니 with no major change in meaning
- The second clause expresses the preferred (though still undesirable) option

### Examples
#### Basic usage
- 적성에 맞지 않는 일을 하면서 마음고생을 할 바에야 차라리 몸이 힘든 일을 하는 게 낫겠어요
	- I would rather do a physically stressful job than suffer mentally while doing work that is not suited to my aptitude

- 뭐든지 꾸준히 해야지. 중간에 하다가 그만둘 바에야 아예 처음부터 안 하는 게 나아
	- Whatever the task is, you must be consistent. It's better not to do it from the beginning than to quit in the middle

- 채소를 비싸게 사 먹을 바에야 번거로워도 직접 집에서 길러 먹어야겠어요
	- Although it is troublesome, I would rather grow vegetables at home than buy expensive vegetables to eat

#### With 차라리 (emphasizing preference)
- 식은 라면을 먹을 바에야 그냥 안 먹을게요
	- I would rather not eat cold ramen

- 학교 식당에서 먹을 바에야 차라리 굶겠다
	- I would rather starve than eat at the school cafeteria

- 항복할 바에야 차라리 죽겠다
	- I would rather die than surrender

- 나는 도둑질을 할 바에야 차라리 굶어 죽겠다
	- I would rather starve to death than steal

#### With 바에는
- 나중에 후회할 바에는 그냥 안 하는 게 낫겠다
	- I would rather not do it than regret it later

- 택시를 탈 바에는 차라리 걸어가는 게 낫겠다
	- I would rather walk than take a taxi

#### Strong preferences
- 그 사람이랑 결혼할 바에야 평생 혼자 사는 게 낫다
	- I would rather live alone for the whole life than marry that person

### Notes
- This pattern emphasizes dissatisfaction with both options but makes a clear choice
- The chosen option (second clause) is still not ideal, but better than the first
- Often used with 차라리, 그냥, or 아예 to strengthen the contrast
- Similar to English "I'd rather... than..." or "Better to... than..."
- Interchangeable with -느니 grammar pattern
?end
