## 자 vs 자마자 #card
?begin
### Usage
Both express "as soon as" or "right after", but with important differences:

**자:**
- Emphasizes causation: the first action causes or motivates the second
- The speaker becomes aware of the second situation as a result of the first action
- Mainly used in writing
- Both clauses must be related

**자마자:**
- Emphasizes temporal sequence: simply indicates timing without causation
- Expresses a faster/more immediate transition between two actions
- Can be used in speech and writing
- Clauses don't need causal relationship

### Form
**자:**
- Verb stem + 자
- Always in present tense form

**자마자:**
- Verb stem + 자마자
- Always in present tense form

### Restrictions
**자:**
- Cannot be used with imperative forms (commands)
- Cannot be used with propositive forms (suggestions)
- Both clauses must be causally related
- Mainly used in writing/formal contexts

**자마자:**
- CAN be used with imperative forms
- CAN be used with propositive forms
- Clauses only need temporal relationship
- More versatile in usage

### Examples
#### 자 - Causal relationship (first causes second)
- 문을 열자 밖에서 기다리는 사람들이 안으로 들어온다
	- As soon as the door opens, people waiting outside come in
- 바람이 불자 비가 온다
	- As soon as the wind blows, it rains
- 까마귀 날자 배 떨어진다
	- As soon as the crow flies, the pear falls (Korean proverb: coincidence)
- 빵을 먹자 배가 아프다
	- As soon as I ate the bread, my stomach hurt
- 창문을 열자 상쾌한 바람이 들어왔다
	- As soon as I opened the window, refreshing air came in
- 버스에서 내리자 비가 오기 시작했다
	- It began to rain as soon as I got off the bus
- 날이 어두워지자 북적거렸던 상가 안이 한산해졌다
	- As soon as the day got dark, the crowded shopping mall became quiet
- 회사에 대한 안 좋은 소문이 나자 그 회사의 주가가 폭락했다
	- When bad news about the company spread, the company's stock price plummeted

#### 자 - Implies speaker's awareness
- 밖에 나오자 눈이 내리기 시작했다
	- After going outside, (I realized that) it has started snowing
	- (Speaker became aware of the snow after going out)

#### 자마자 - Pure temporal sequence
- 밖에 나오자마자 눈이 내리기 시작했다
	- Right after going outside, it simply started snowing
	- (Just describing the timing, no emphasis on causation)

#### 자마자 - Can be used when actions are unrelated
- 모자를 사자 잃어버렸다 ❌ (wrong - no causal relationship)
- 모자를 사자마자 잃어버렸다 ✓ (correct - just timing)
	- I lost the hat right after buying it

#### 자마자 - Can be used with commands/suggestions
- 집에서 나가자 택시를 타십시오 ❌ (wrong)
- 집에서 나가자마자 택시를 타십시오 ✓ (correct)
	- Take a taxi as soon as you leave the house

- 집에서 나가자 택시를 탑시다 ❌ (wrong)
- 집에서 나가자마자 택시를 탑시다 ✓ (correct)
	- Let's take a taxi as soon as we leave the house

### Notes
**Key Differences Summary:**
1. **Relationship**: 자 requires causal/motivational relationship; 자마자 only requires temporal sequence
2. **Speed**: 자마자 implies faster/more immediate transition
3. **Awareness**: 자 can imply the speaker's realization; 자마자 is purely descriptive
4. **Grammar compatibility**: 자마자 works with imperatives and propositives; 자 does not
5. **Usage context**: 자 is more literary/formal; 자마자 is more versatile

**Note:** 자 also has a separate usage as the informal form of -읍시다 (let's do), used at the end of sentences with friends:
- 밥을 먹자 = Let's eat
- 공부하자 = Let's study
- 같이 마시자 = Let's drink together

This "let's" usage is unrelated to the "as soon as" meaning discussed above.
?end
