These three verbs all relate to "knowing" or "finding out," but they differ in nuance and usage:

---

**알아보다** — _to look into / to check / to find out (by investigating)_

This implies an **active process of researching or inquiring**. The focus is on the _effort_ of seeking information.

> 제가 알아볼게요. — _I'll look into it / I'll check for you._ 호텔 가격을 알아봤어요. — _I checked hotel prices._

It can also mean **to recognize** someone/something:

> 오래됐는데 알아봤어요? — _It's been a long time — did you recognize me?_

---

**알아채다** — _to notice / to sense / to catch on_

This is about **suddenly perceiving or detecting** something, often something subtle or hidden. It has a sense of **instinctive or perceptive awareness**.

> 거짓말인 걸 알아챘어요. — _I caught on that it was a lie._ 아무도 알아채지 못했어요. — _Nobody noticed._

It often implies the thing detected was _not obvious_ — you picked up on a clue.

---

**알아내다** — _to figure out / to discover / to uncover_

This emphasizes **reaching a conclusion or answer** after effort. The focus is on the **result** — you successfully found the answer.

> 범인을 알아냈어요. — _I figured out who the culprit was._ 비밀번호를 알아냈어요. — _I found out the password._

---

**Quick comparison:**

|Verb|Core meaning|Focus|
|---|---|---|
|알아보다|to look into, investigate|the _process_ of searching|
|알아채다|to notice, sense|a _sudden perception_ of something subtle|
|알아내다|to figure out, uncover|the _result_ of successfully finding an answer|

A helpful scenario: if someone is hiding a surprise party —

- 알아**보다**: you're actively asking around to find out what's going on
- 알아**채다**: you suddenly _sense_ something is off from small hints
- 알아**내다**: you've successfully _figured out_ that it's a surprise party
