#### -(으)ㄹ 텐데

This expresses the speaker's **assumption or expectation** — you are fairly confident about what you think will happen:

- **안 올 텐데 걱정했어요**
- "I _figured/assumed_ you wouldn't come, so I was worried"
- The speaker is leaning toward believing it will happen

---

#### -(으)ㄹ까 봐

This expresses **fear or worry about a possibility** — you are not sure it will happen but you are anxious about it:

- **안 올까 봐 걱정했어요**
- "I was worried you _might_ not come"
- The speaker sees it as a possibility they are nervous about, not necessarily expecting

---

#### In your specific sentence:

**안 올까 봐 걱정했어요** is actually the **more natural choice** here, because:

- You were _worried_ — which implies uncertainty and anxiety, not a firm assumption
- **텐데** carries a stronger sense of "I was pretty sure you wouldn't come" which makes worrying feel slightly contradictory — if you were that certain, the emotion would be more like disappointment than worry

---

So in short:

- **텐데** = "I expected/assumed"
- **을까 봐** = "I was scared/worried that maybe"
