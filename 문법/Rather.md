Here are the key Korean words meaning "rather" or "instead," compared:

---

**차라리** — _rather, might as well, if anything_ Used when choosing the lesser of two evils, or expressing a preference that's somewhat reluctant or resigned.

- 차라리 죽겠다 — "I'd rather die (than do that)"
- 차라리 집에 있을게 — "I might as well just stay home"

Has an emotional weight to it — often implies the alternative is unpleasant or the speaker is exasperated.

---

**대신 (에)** — _instead of, in place of_ More neutral substitution. One thing replaces another.

- 커피 대신 차를 마셨다 — "I drank tea instead of coffee"
- 내 대신 가줘 — "Go in my place / instead of me"

No emotional coloring, just replacement.

---

**오히려** — _rather, on the contrary, if anything_ Used when something is the opposite of what was expected. Closer to "actually" or "if anything."

- 약이 오히려 더 나쁠 수 있다 — "The medicine can actually make things worse"
- 오히려 내가 미안해 — "If anything, _I'm_ the one who's sorry"

---

**그보다는 / 그것보다** — _rather than that_ More conversational, used to pivot to a preferred option.

- 그보다는 이게 낫지 않아? — "Isn't this better than that?"

---

**Quick comparison:**

|Word|Nuance|
|---|---|
|차라리|Reluctant preference, "might as well"|
|대신|Neutral replacement|
|오히려|Contrary to expectation|
|그보다는|Casual pivot to preference|

The biggest trap for learners is mixing up **차라리** and **오히려** — 차라리 is about _choosing_, 오히려 is about _surprise or contrast_.
