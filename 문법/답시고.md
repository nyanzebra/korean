## 답시고 #card
?begin
### Usage
1. **Main usage (Action Verbs only)**: The person intends to do something in the first clause but ends up failing or even making it worse, which is sarcastically or ironically pointed out.
2. **Contrasting usage**: Whatever happens or is claimed in the first clause is proven inadequate or unsatisfactory in the second clause.
3. **Reason usage**: The first clause is the reason for the result in the second clause, but the result is out of proportion (expressing disapproval).
4. **Modest usage**: Express one's own action with modesty.

### Form
**Action Verbs:**
- Consonant ending: Verb stem + 는답시고
- Vowel ending: Verb stem + ㄴ답시고

**Descriptive Verbs/Adjectives:**
- Verb stem + 다고

**Nouns:**
- (이)랍시고

**With assumption (겠):**
- Verb stem + 겠답시고

### Restrictions
- Creates an ironic or sarcastic tone
- Often expresses disapproval or unsatisfactory results
- Usage 1 requires action verbs only
- Often contrasts intent with result

### Examples
#### Usage 1 - Sarcastic intent vs. failed result
- 엄마가 내 방을 청소를 한답시고 내 핸드폰을 버렸어
	- My mom was going to clean my room but ended up throwing away my phone
- 케이크를 만들겠답시고 만들어봤는데 못했어요
	- I thought I could make a cake, but I tried and it turned out that I couldn't

#### Usage 2-1 - Contrast/inadequacy
- 머리가 좋답시고 TV를 켰는데 못켰어
	- I'm really smart, but when I turned on the TV, I couldn't do it
- 요새는 바쁘답시고 책을 많이 못 읽는다
	- I'm busy these days so I can't really read a lot of books
- 생일선물이랍시고 오래된 케이크를 주었다
	- I gave an old cake and called it a birthday present

#### Usage 2-2 - Out of proportion reason
- 저보다 나이가 많답시고 가르치는 식으로 말하는 놈이에요
	- He's the type of person who speaks to me like he's trying to teach me something just because he's older than me

#### Usage 3 - Modesty
- 난 정말 열심히 공부한답시고 했는데 잘 된지 모르겠다
	- I studied really hard, but I don't know if it went well

### Notes
All usages have common elements:
- Unsatisfying result or disapproval
- Contrast between clauses
- Ironic or sarcastic undertone
