## 주셔서 & 계셔서 #card
?begin
### Usage
#### 계셔서

From **계시다** — the honorific form of **있다** (to be / to exist / to stay)

> Honors the subject's **state or presence**

|Example|Meaning|
|---|---|
|여기 계셔서 다행이에요|I'm glad you are here|
|함께 계셔서 감사합니다|Thank you for being with us|

The gratitude/feeling is directed at the person simply **existing or being present** in that state.

---

#### 주셔서

From **주다** (to give) → honorific **주시다** → **주셔서**

But the key is that **~아/어 주다** means "to do something _for someone's benefit_"

> Honors the subject's **action done for you**

|Example|Meaning|
|---|---|
|도와주셔서 감사합니다|Thank you for helping me|
|와주셔서 감사합니다|Thank you for coming (for my sake)|
|말씀해 주셔서 감사해요|Thank you for telling me|
### Notes

#### Side by side

|             | 계셔서                      | 주셔서                             |
| ----------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------------- |
| **Base**    | 계시다 (honorific 있다)       | 주시다 (honorific 주다)              |
| **Focus**   | Being / presence / state | An action done for your benefit |
| **Pattern** | 계셔서 + feeling            | Verb + 아/어 주셔서 + feeling        |

---

#### The subtle difference in gratitude

- **계셔서 감사해요** → "I'm grateful that you _are here_" — your presence itself is what I appreciate
- **와 주셔서 감사해요** → "I'm grateful that you _came_ (for me)" — your action on my behalf is what I appreciate

**주셔서** has a nuance of the person going out of their way to do something for you, while **계셔서** is more about their state or presence being appreciated.
?end
