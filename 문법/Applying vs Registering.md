You're probably thinking of **접수하다**! Here are all three:

---

**신청하다** — to apply for / to register interest in something:

- 수업을 신청했어요 — I signed up for a class
- 장학금을 신청했어요 — I applied for a scholarship
- 비자를 신청했어요 — I applied for a visa

The nuance is that you're **expressing intent or making a request** — the outcome isn't guaranteed. You're putting your name forward for something.

---

**등록하다** — to register / to enroll:

- 학교에 등록했어요 — I enrolled in school
- 회원으로 등록했어요 — I registered as a member
- 수업에 등록했어요 — I enrolled in the class

More official and confirmed than 신청 — you're **formally entering yourself** into a system. Often involves payment or official documentation.

---

**접수하다** — to check in / to submit for processing:

- 병원에서 접수했어요 — I checked in at the hospital
- 서류를 접수했어요 — I submitted the documents
- 접수처가 어디예요? — where is the reception/check-in desk?

This is your hospital word — it specifically means **submitting yourself or documents to be processed**. At hospitals it's the check-in before seeing the doctor.

---

**The progression often goes:** 신청 → 등록 → 접수 apply → enroll → check in

So you might 신청 for a class, then 등록 when confirmed, then 접수 your documents when you arrive!
