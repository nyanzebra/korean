## N(이)나 #card
?begin
### Usage
Express choice or amount of something
### Form
명사 + (이)나
### Restrictions
### Examples
#### Or
* 이참에 빵이나 라면을 먹어요.
	* I eat bread or noodle in the morning.
* 방학에 호주나 유럽에 가고 싶어요.
	* I want to go to the Australia or Europe during the school vacation.
* 어머니나 아버지가 요리해요.
	* My mother or father cooks.
#### As much as
* 친구를 두 시간이나 기다렸어요.
	* I waited for my friend for no less than 2 hours.
* 우리 할머니는 아이가 열 명이나 있어요.
	* Our grandmother has up to 10 children
* 친구에게 5번이나 전화했어요.
	* I phoned my friend no less than 5 times.
### Notes
https://www.koreantopik.com/2017/12/l1g14-n-n-n-grammar-orno-less-than.html

This is different to `넘게` in that instead of indicating 'going over' some value, it went 'up to' instead.

----
#### 한 시간 넘게 기다렸잖아

- **넘게** — "more than, over" (from 넘다)
- Emphasizes that it **exceeded** one hour
- Feels more factual/objective
- _"We waited for over an hour, you know."_

---

#### 한 시간이나 기다렸잖아

- **이나** — "as much as, a whole" (marks an amount as surprisingly large)
- Emphasizes **emotional reaction** — disbelief, frustration, complaint
- _"We waited a whole hour, you know!"_

?end
