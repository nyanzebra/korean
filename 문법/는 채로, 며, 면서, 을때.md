## ~는 채로 vs ~며/면서/을 때 #card
?begin
### Usage
These all relate to simultaneous or concurrent situations, but they have important differences.
### Notes

#### ~는 채로

**"While still in that state / As-is / Without changing from that state"**

The key idea is that a **prior state is maintained unchanged** while another action happens. It often implies something is left as-is (sometimes carelessly or unexpectedly).

|Example|Meaning|
|---|---|
|신발을 신은 채로 들어왔어요|Came in while still wearing shoes (didn't take them off)|
|불을 켠 채로 잠들었어요|Fell asleep with the light still on|
|옷을 입은 채로 수영했어요|Swam while still fully clothed|
|눈을 감은 채로 걸었어요|Walked with eyes kept closed|

> ⚠️ Always attaches to a **past/resultant state**: **~(으)ㄴ 채로** (not ~는 채로 for most verbs)

---

#### ~며 / ~면서

**"While doing / At the same time as"**

Two actions happen **simultaneously by the same subject**. The actions are ongoing together — no special emphasis on a prior state being maintained.

|Example|Meaning|
|---|---|
|음악을 들으면서 공부해요|Study while listening to music|
|웃으며 말했어요|Said it while smiling|
|걸으면서 전화했어요|Talked on the phone while walking|

> ~며 is slightly more formal/literary; ~면서 is more common in speech.

---

#### ~을 때

**"When / At the time of"**

Refers to a **point in time or period** when something happens. It's about timing, not necessarily simultaneity.

|Example|Meaning|
|---|---|
|어릴 때 피아노를 쳤어요|Played piano when I was young|
|비가 올 때 집에 있었어요|Was at home when it rained|
|밥을 먹을 때 TV를 봐요|Watch TV when eating|

#### Side-by-side comparison

| |~(으)ㄴ 채로|~면서|~을 때|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Core idea**|Maintaining a prior state|Two simultaneous actions|A point/period in time|
|**Focus**|State unchanged|Concurrent activity|Timing|
|**Subject**|Same subject|Same subject|Can differ|
|**Nuance**|"still in that state"|"at the same time"|"at that moment/period"|

#### Easy way to remember

- **채로** → _"as-is," nothing changed_ → 신발을 신은 채로 (shoes still on)
- **면서** → _"simultaneously doing both"_ → 먹으면서 말하다 (eating AND talking)
- **을 때** → _"at that time"_ → 어릴 때 (back when I was young)

?end
