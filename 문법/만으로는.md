## 만으로는 #card
?begin
### Usage
Expresses "only with...", "only on the basis of...", "using only..." with the nuance that this alone is insufficient. This pattern combines three particles (만 + 으로 + 는) and emphasizes that using ONLY the noun mentioned won't be enough.

### Form
**Noun + 만으로는**
- Only nouns, or verbs conjugated into nouns, can be used
- Can drop 는 to use just 만으로 (allows positive/neutral sentences too)

**Particle breakdown:**
1. 만 - only
2. 으로 - using / by the means of / with
3. 는 - contrast marker/emphasis marker

### Restrictions
- When using 만으로는 (with 는), the following clause MUST be negative
- Common negative endings include:
  - 가능하지 않다 (not possible)
  - 부족하다 (insufficient)
  - 못하다 (cannot)
  - -ㄹ 수 없다 (cannot)
  - -기 어렵다 (difficult to)

- Without 는 (just 만으로), can use positive, neutral, or negative clauses

### Examples
#### With 만으로는 (negative following clause required)
- 사랑만으로는 결혼할 수 없다
	- You can't get married only on the basis of love
- 수업 시간만으로는 모든 것을 다 배우지 못한다
	- You can't learn everything just during class hours
- 라면만으로는 살 수 없다
	- You can't live only on ramyeon
- 쌍꺼풀 수술만으로는 부족하다
	- Only double eyelid surgery is not enough
- 첫인상만으로는 그 사람이 어떤 사람인지 알 수 없다
	- You can't know what kind of person he/she is only from the first impression

#### With 만으로 (positive/neutral/negative all okay)
- 사랑만으로 결혼할 수 있다
	- You can get married only on the basis of love

### Notes
The particle 는 at the end creates emphasis that what is mentioned is insufficient alone, requiring something additional. This is why negative expressions naturally follow.
