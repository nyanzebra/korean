## 말고 #card
?begin
### Usage
Used to express "instead of", "not... but", or "don't do... instead". It negates the first option and suggests or implies an alternative. Often used in commands, suggestions, or contrasts.

### Form
**With Nouns:**
- Noun + 말고

**With Verbs (commands/suggestions):**
- Verb stem + 지 말고

### Restrictions
- Cannot be used with past tense
- Typically used in imperative or suggestive sentences
- The alternative action/option is usually stated after 말고
- 말고 can be followed by 도 to add emphasis ("not only... but also")

### Examples
#### With Nouns (instead of)
- 저녁에 고기말고 야채만 먹었어요
	- For dinner I didn't eat meat, only ate veggies
- 한국에서 여성 화장품말고 남성 화장품도 많아요
	- In Korea there are not only female cosmetics but lots of male cosmetics also
- 이거 말고 저거 주세요
	- Not this one, give me that one instead
- 커피 말고 차를 마실게요
	- Instead of coffee, I'll drink tea

#### With Verbs (don't do... instead)
- 여드름이 나면 짜지 말고 약을 바르세요
	- If acne shows up, don't pop it, apply medicine instead
- 택시 타지 말고 걸어가자
	- Don't take a taxi, let's walk instead
- 울지 말고 웃어요
	- Don't cry, smile instead
- 집에서 쉬지 말고 운동하세요
	- Don't rest at home, exercise instead
- 거짓말하지 말고 사실을 말해
	- Don't lie, tell the truth

### Notes
- When used with nouns, 말고 directly follows the noun
- When used with verbs, requires -지 말고 form
- Often implies the speaker's preference or advice
- The second clause (after 말고) can be implicit if context is clear
- Similar to -지 말고 which is specifically for negative commands with alternatives
?end
